The God of Glory Appeared to Abraham and Called Him

Steven calls the One who appeared to Abraham, sending to the Land of Canaan, the God of Glory.

"The God of glory appeared to our father Abraham while he was still in Mesopotamia, before he lived in Haran." [Act 7:2]

Abraham Knew Jesus

According to Jesus, he and Abraham had met during Abraham's lifetime. In fact, Jesus states that he gave a word to Abraham and that Abraham believed him and looked forward with eager expectation to the fulfillment of the word.

This word, according to Jesus was fulfilled in Abraham's lifetime.

“Your father Abraham rejoiced at the thought of seeing my day; he saw it and was glad.” [Joh 8:56]

(We know that this was what Jesus meant because this was how the Jews, as recorded by John, understood him. They understood Jesus' words to have meant that he had personally met with Abraham, for they asked,

“You are not yet fifty years old... and you have seen Abraham!” [Joh 8:57]

While there is the possibility that they misunderstood Jesus, neither John nor Jesus corrected their understanding of Jesus' words as faulty. In fact, we can see from Jesus' response to their objection that they were not wrong in their understanding of his words for Jesus answered,

“I tell you the truth,” Jesus answered, “before Abraham was born, I am!”[Joh 8:58])

So, Where Did Abraham and Jesus Meet?

Now, there is only one word that was

  1. Given to Abraham in his lifetime, and which was
  2. Fulfilled in his lifetime and
  3. Which Abraham desired and looked forward to.
This was the word Yahweh gave him and Sarah concerning the birth of Isaac when Yahweh came in the company of two angels the day Sodom and Gomorrah was destroyed.

Jesus Was Yahweh

Of the three men--Yahweh and the two angels--only Yahweh spoke the word of Isaac's conception to Abraham [Gen 18:10].

This Was Not Their First Meeting

This was certainly not their first meeting. We can plainly see this from
  1. Abraham's going out to greet them and his words to them.
    • “If I have found favor in your eyes, my lord, do not pass your servant by.
      Let a little water be brought, and then you may all wash your feet and rest under this tree.
      Let me get you something to eat, so you can be refreshed and then go on your way--now that you have come to your servant.” [Gen 18:3-5]
  2. His interaction with and words to Yahweh in his intercession from Sodom and Gomorrah which showed a measure of familiarity with him
    • Far be it from you to do such a thing--to kill the righteous with the wicked, treating the righteous and the wicked alike. Far be it from you! Will not the Judge of all the earth do right?” [Gen 18:25]
  3. Yahweh's statement that He was the One who had chosen Abraham for Yahweh
    • Then Yahweh said, “...Abraham will surely become a great and powerful nation, and all nations on earth will be blessed through him.
      For I have chosen him, so that he will direct his children and his household after him to keep the way of Yahweh by doing what is right and just, so that Yahweh will bring about for Abraham what he has promised him.” [Gen 18:17-19]


El-Shaddai

When Yahweh made the Covenant of Circumcision with Abraham, He called Himself by the Name El-Shaddai.

When Abram was ninety-nine years old, YAHWEH appeared to him and said, “I am EL-SHADDAI (trans: God Almighty); walk before me and be blameless. 2I will confirm my covenant between me and you and will greatly increase your numbers.” [Gen 17:1-2]

Yahweh, The Angel of Yahweh & El-Shaddai

Jacob, in blessing Joseph referred to the Angel of Yahweh as El-Shaddai.

But his bow remained steady, his strong arms stayed limber, because of the hand of the Mighty One of Jacob, because of the Shepherd, the Rock of Israel, 25because of your father’s God, who helps you, because of the Almighty, who blesses you with blessings of the heavens above, blessings of the deep that lies below, blessings of the breast and womb.  [Gen 49:24,25]

He was

  1. The Face (or Presence) of Yahweh whom Jacob had wrestled with and who changed his name to Israel,
  2. The Shepherd,
  3. Israel’s Rock,
  4. Shaddai,
  5. The One who was Jacob’s Shepherd all his life [Gen 49:24,25 cf., Gen 4:15-16].
