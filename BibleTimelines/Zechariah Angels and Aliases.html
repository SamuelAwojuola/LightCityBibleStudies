Identifying the Angels of Zechariah's Vision

One of the main issues with Zechariah in the first visions is the identity/count of angels. For example, in the first vision is there just one angel under different names or are there two angels?

In this timeline, I try to establish clear connections between one angel and the other and showing them as the same or otherwise.

If two angels fall under the same column, then they are the same.

{% include BStL-detailsSection-Buttons.html %}

The Surveyor Angel and the Angel Who Spoke with Zechariah

The above descriptions in the above in the third vision are of the same angel. We conclude from the following observations,

  1. The Surveyor Angel spoke with Zechariah [Zec 2:2]. Therefore, "the angel who spoke with me" would be referring to the Surveyor Angel.
  2. The surveyor Angel was going away from Zechariah (Zechariah had asked him. "Where are you going?" [Zec 2:2]). The Angel who spoke with Zechariah was also leaving for Zechariah before he was met by another Angel who directed to go back to Zechariah [Zec 2:3].

Just One Angel in the First Vision--02

Yahweh spoke to the Angel who spoke to Zechariah [Zec 1:13] as though he was the one who had petitioned God about Jersualem [Zec 1:12].

This again shows the Angel on the Red Horse to be the same as “the Angel who spoke to...” Zechariah.

Just One Angel in The First Vision--01

The promise of “the Angel who spoke to...” Zechariah (to show Zechariah what the Coloured Horses were [Zec 1:9]) was fulfilled by the Angel of on the Red Horse (who explained what they were to Zechariah [Zec 1:10]).


Involvement and Relationship To God's People

  1. The prayer of the Angel on the Red Horse for Jerusalem and God answering him with "kind and comforting words" shows that he has a personal relationship with Jerusalem and God's people.
  2. In the same way, the Angel of Yahweh before whom Joshua the high priest stood demonstrated a personal involvement with Jerusalem and God's people.
    1. The high priest standing before him and he overseeing his change of clothes shows that the high priest, and so the Law, and so God's people were under him. [Zec 3:1,4]
    2. His rebuke of Satan reveals a personal interest in Jerusalem [Zec 3:1-3
