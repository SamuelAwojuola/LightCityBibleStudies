Where Did The Light Appear?

The light appeared on the surface of the deep

  1. First, since the darkness was stated to be upon the surface of the deep, when God commands that there should be light, one would naturally expect that the light was supposed to be where the darkness was.
  2. The separation of light and darkness was time--each had its time. This means that they affected the same region.
  3. Also, the fact that there was evening, which is darkness, and morning, which is light, also means that they affected the same region or else they could not be measured as evening and morning.
  4. Therefore, since the darkness was on the surface of the deep, that is the water below, as quite plainly stated, it means that the light shined on the surface of the deep as well.

Also, Paul speaks of the light shinning out of darkness,

For God, who said, “Let light shine out of darkness,” made his light shine in our hearts to give us the light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Christ. [2Cor 4:6].

What Was The Source of the Light?

The source of the light was either of the following,

  1. The sun, which would mean that the sun was created on the first day but...
    1. The thick clouds, i.e., the waters above, blocked out the light of the sun [Job 36:32] and the stars [Job 3:5-6,9]
  2. The Spirit of God upon the face of the waters (of the deep).
    1. In the wilderness, God's glory appeared in the cloud [Exo 16:10; Psa 78:14], therefore, the Spirit of God could have illuminated the waters above the deep and thus shone light.
    2. Also, in the Millennial reign and in the Kingdom proper, the glory of God will literally light Israel and the world so that they don't rely on the light of the sun or moon but on the light of the Glory of God [Isa 60:19-20; Rev 21:24; 22:5; Isa 24:23]

Creation of the Elements?

It must be noted that the creation account of Genesis starts with already existing materials--darkness and waters and an earth under the waters. We are not told how or when God created base materials we are only told how God structured it and made new things from the already pre-existing.

Where Was the Holy Spirit?

The Holy Spirit was upon the waters [Gen 1:2], but which water?

  1. Is this both the water above and the water below? Or
  2. Is this just either of the waters below or the waters above?
And the earth was without form, and void; and darkness was upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters. [Gen 1:2 KJV]

Everything that followed is presented as happening under the waters above

  1. The light shining on the surface of the deep where the darkness was is under the waters above
  2. The expanse between the waters below and the waters above is under the waters above
  3. The gathering of the waters and the appearing of the land and green vegetation, the creation of the fish in the waters, the birds in the air, the animals on land and finally man all happened under the waters above.
  4. Even the sun, moon and stars are stated as appearing in the expanse between the waters above the waters below.

Therefore, the waters upon which the Spirit rested must have been the waters below. From the start, nothing was said about the waters above but only about the waters below. And nothing happened in or above the waters above.

What was the Holy Spirit Doing?

Apparently, the Spirit of God was the one who carried out or fulfilled the creation commands of God.

The scriptures attribute the acts of God's power to His Spirit.

God Lives Above The Waters of Heaven

He wraps himself in light as with a garment; he stretches out the heavens like a tent 3and lays the beams of his upper chambers on their waters. [Psa 104:2-3]

The waters in the above quotation are the waters that are above and not the waters below.

So, God's "upper chamber" is above the waters above.
