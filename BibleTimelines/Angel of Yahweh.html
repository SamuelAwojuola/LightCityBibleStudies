Whatever his role was, the Angel of Yahweh was certainly present at the creation of the world.

Proof

  1. Angels were present at the Creation of the World. One of the reasons for this is the fact we learn from Yahweh that the angels, sons of God, the morning stars, were present at the creation of the world [Job 38:7].
  2. Yahweh, the Angel of Yahweh's Name. The Name of the Angel of Yahweh points to him being the one who created all things. His Name, Yahweh, means the One who brings things into being. This points to him being the Creator of the world. In fact, the creation of the world started with 'Hayah'---"Let there be" [Gen 1:2]

The "God of Glory" Appeared to Abraham 

"The God of glory appeared to our father Abraham while he was still in Mesopotamia, before he lived in Haran." [Act 7:2]

Why Consider This to Be the Angel of Yahweh.

  1. The testimony of the Jesus and the Apostles that "No one has ever seen God at any time" [Joh 1:18; 5:37; 1Ti 1:17] implies that this was not God in very person but a representation of Him.
  2. The same One who appeared to Abraham as "El-Shaddai" appeared to Jacob as well and is later revealed to be the "Angel of Yahweh"  [Gen 35:11; 48:3 cf., Gen 31:11; 48:15-16].
    Reading the story of Abraham up to the point where God reveals Himself to Abraham as 'El-Shaddai" [Gen 17:1], it is clear, from the unbroken narrative, that El-Shaddai is the same one who appeared to him while he was still in Mesopotamia and sent him to the land of Canaan. Therefore, that we later learn that the Angel of Yahweh is "El-Shaddai" means that he was in fact the One who had appeared to Abraham.
    Looking ahead and learning from Jacob that the Angel of Yahweh is "El-Shaddai", we conclude that He was in fact the One who had appeared to Abraham while he was still living in Mesopotamia.

First Meeting of Moses and the Angel of Yahweh

Moses' first meeting of the Angel of Yahweh was on Mount Sinai in Median.

When Moses first met the Angel of Yahweh, the Angel referred to himself as Yahweh and not as the Angel of Yahweh. It was the author of Exodus who alone referred to the Angel as the Angel of Yahweh, but the Angel himself never did.
