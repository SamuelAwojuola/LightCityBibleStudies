Sin Is Crouching At the Door

Sin is at the door. It desires to have you, but you must master it.”[Gen 4:6-7]

At the time when God spoke these words to Cain, Adam's family, along with Cain were living in Eden outside of the Garden. Now, since Eden with its Garden was a tabernacle, Eden had two main doors, using the tabernacle language

  1. The door to the courtyard, which is Eden itself, and
  2. The door to the tabernacle, which is the Garden of Eden

Now, since they were living outside of the Garden, the door to which God would have been referring could therefore only have been the outer door, the door in and out of Eden itself.

The door of the Garden of Eden was guarded by Cherubim and so could not have been a place where sin was crouching.

The door where sin was crouching was the door of Eden, sin was therefore outside of Eden at the door longing to rule over Cain, waiting to be Lord over anyone who would be cast out of Eden.

What God is saying is quite clear and it is that if Cain did not do what was right, he would be cast out of Eden into the hands of sin who had been crouching, and waiting outside of Eden to master Cain or any other man that would be cast out of Eden.

If God is referring to Satan as Sin, then His words to Cain imply that Satan, aka, sin, was outside the Land of Eden.
