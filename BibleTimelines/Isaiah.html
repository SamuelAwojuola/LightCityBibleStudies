Isaiah 6

In the year that King Uzziah died, I saw Adonai seated on a throne, high and exalted, and the train of his robe filled the temple.

2Above him were seraphs, each with six wings: With two wings they covered their faces, with two they covered their feet, and with two they were flying. 3And they were calling to one another: “Holy, holy, holy is Yahweh Sabaoth; the whole earth is full of his glory.” 4At the sound of their voices the doorposts and thresholds shook and the temple was filled with smoke.

5“Woe to me!” I cried. “I am ruined! For I am a man of unclean lips, and I live among a people of unclean lips, and my eyes have seen the King, Yahweh Sabaoth.”

6Then one of the seraphs flew to me with a live coal in his hand, which he had taken with tongs from the altar. 7With it he touched my mouth and said, “See, this has touched your lips; your guilt is taken away and your sin atoned for.”

8Then I heard the voice of Adonai saying, “Whom shall I send? And who will go for us?”

And I said, “Here am I. Send me!”

9He said, “Go and tell this people:

“‘Be ever hearing, but never understanding;
be ever seeing, but never perceiving.’
10Make the heart of this people calloused;
make their ears dull and close their eyes.
Otherwise they might see with their eyes, hear with their ears, understand with their hearts, and turn and be healed.”

11Then I said, “For how long, O Adonai?”

And he answered: “Until the cities lie ruined and without inhabitant, until the houses are left deserted and the fields ruined and ravaged, 12until Yahweh has sent everyone far away and the land is utterly forsaken. 13And though a tenth remains in the land, it will again be laid waste. But as the terebinth and oak leave stumps when they are cut down, so the holy seed will be the stump in the land.” [Isa 6:1-13]

The Last Days

Zion and the "Mountain of Yahweh's Temple" in the Last Days

  1. Zion Becomes "The City of Righteousness, The Faithful City"
    1. Yahweh will restore Zion's judges [Isa 1:26]
    2. People everywhere acknowledge Zion as righteous and faithful to Yahweh and begin to call her "The City of Righteousness, The Faithful City" [Isa 1:26]
  2. Exaltation of Temple Mount
    1. "The Mountain of Yahweh's Temple" will be exalted as Chief among mountains [Isa 2:2]
  3. Many Nations Will Flow To The Mountain
    1. All nations will stream to the "Mountain of Yahweh's Temple" [Isa 2:2,3]
  4. They Will Come To Learn The Ways of Yahweh
    1. The nations and many peoples will come to the "Mountain of Yahweh's Temple" to come and learn the ways of Yahweh so that they may walk in them [Isa 2:2,3]
  5. Peace Among Nations [Isa 2:3,4]
    1. The Law of the nations of the earth will be designed in Zion [Isa 2:3]
    2. Yahweh will judge between nations and settle disputes for many peoples [Isa 2:4]
    3. They will cease to fight against each other [Isa 2:4]

Zion's Redemption

Yahweh’s plan to restore Jerusalem to righteousness is to

  1. To bring Jerusalem as a city to repentance,
    1. Through wrath and judgment [Isa 1:25,27,31] and
    2. The preaching of the Gospel [Isa 40]
  2. Remove the wicked in her [Isa 1:25,28,31],
  3. Preserve the repentant and penitent ones in her [Isa 1:2] and
  4. Give her righteous leaders [Isa 1:26].

The Fate of Idols

Yahweh will bring an end to idolatry in all the earth in the Day of Yahweh [Isa 1:10-21]. 

"...Yahweh alone will be exalted in that day, 18and the idols will totally disappear." [Isa 1:7-18]

The Day of Yahweh (The LORD)

  1. Men will see Yahweh in glorious splendor [Isa 2:10,19]
  2. Men will be terrified by Yahweh's splendor and will
    1. Try to hide from Yahweh [Isa 2:10,19]
    2. Throw away their idols [Isa 2:20]
  3. Yahweh will shake the earth [Isa 2:19]
    1. The fact that men try to hide in the rocks and caves [Isa 2:10,19,21] and not in their houses shows that the shaking of the earth by Yahweh includes a literal shaking of physical things.
    2. apparently, the reason why they don't try to hide in their houses is because they will not be safe to dwell in on that day.
  4. Idolatry will cease [Isa 1:7-18,10-21]
  5. Men will be destroyed
    1. We can see this from God speaking of "survivors in Israel" [Isa 4:2,3]
