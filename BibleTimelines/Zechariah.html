Guiding Angel Not the Angel of Yahweh

This timeline tests to see if the Guiding Angel and the Angel of Yahweh are different.

It tries to follow the narrative with them as different angels.

The Angel of Yahweh, hearing the report that the world is at peace, prays to Yahweh of Hosts for Jerusalem.

“Lord Almighty, how long will you withhold mercy from Jerusalem and from the towns of Judah, which you have been angry with these seventy years?” [Zec 1:12]

The Angel of Yahweh Rebukes Satan

Zec 3:1-3
  1. Joshua the High Priest, dressed in filthy clothes [Zec 3:4] was standing before the Angel of Yahweh in order that he may be honored (as can be seen from the rest of the vision and from the fact that Satan was standing to accuse him which would only happen if the accused was being considered for some honor)
  2. Satan stands at the right hand of Joshua the High Priest to accuse him
  3. Yahweh, i.e., the Angel of Yahweh rebukes Satan in the Name of Yahweh who chose Jerusalem

The Angel of Yahweh in This Vision

  1. The Angel of Yahweh was called “Yahweh”. 
    1. Apparently, therefore, he is the same Angel of Yahweh we read of in Genesis and Exodus
  2. He is apparently the One who called for Joshua to be honored. Therefore,
    1. Since Joshua was the High Priest of Israel, he had to be the Angel of the Covenant whom God set over Israel
    2. This also makes him the Captain of the Hosts of Israel, the One whom God set over Israel.
  3. The Angel in the first vision, the Angel sitting on the red horse to whom the horses reported was also called the Angel of Yahweh.
    1. The Angel's involvement with Jerusalem and God's people, as revealed in his prayer to God for them, meant that he was the Angel God appointed over Israel.
    2. This means that the Angel of Yahweh among the Myrtle Trees is the same person as the Angel of Yahweh in this vision before whom Joshua the High Priest was standing

The Angel of Yahweh Gives A Charge to Joshua the High Priest

Zec 3:6-7

After changing the filthy clothes of Joshua the High Priest to Rich Garments with a clean turban on his head, the Angel of Yahweh gives a charge to Joshua the High Priest.

If Joshua walks in the ways of Yahweh and keeps His requirements then

  1. Joshua will govern the house of Yahweh and
  2. He will have charge of his courts(this is similar to the exaltation Joseph received from Pharaoh--“You shall be in charge of my palace, and all my people are to submit to your orders. Only with respect to the throne will I be greater than you.” [Gen 41:40])
  3. He will be given “a place among these standing here”

The Angel of Yahweh Gives A Charge to Joshua the High Priest

Now Joshua was dressed in filthy clothes as he stood before the angel.
4The angel said to those who were standing before him, “Take off his filthy clothes.” Then he said to Joshua, “See, I have taken away your sin, and I will put rich garments on you.”
5Then I said, “Put a clean turban on his head.” So they put a clean turban on his head and clothed him, while the Angel of Yahweh stood by. [Zec 3:3-5]

Breakdown of the Above Verses

  1. Joshua the High Priest was dressed in filthy clothes [Zec 3:3]
    1. These filthy clothes represent his sin [Zec 3:4]
  2. At the command of the Angel of Yahweh, his filthy clothes were taken off [Zec 3:4]
    1. The filthy clothes were removed in order that rich garments may be put on him
  3. The Prophet Zechariah requested that a clean turban be put on Joshua's head [Zec 3:5]
  4. Joshua was dressed in the rich garments with the clean turban on his head [Zec 3:5]

The Stone with Seven Eyes, A.K.A The Branch

Zec 3:8-10

The Angel of Yahweh speaks not only to High Priest Joshua but also to his associates seated before him

  1. They are symbolic of things to come [Zec 3:8]
    1. By this we can see that this prophecy is not simply about the Joshua the High Priest. Rather, these men are used to represent greater matters.
  2. God announces that he is going to bring “My Servant the Branch” [Zec 3:8]
  3. There is a Stone with Seven Eyes
    1. Apparently, this Stone is the same as the Branch in the previous verse
    2. God says that He will engrave an inscription on the Stone and this will result in the removal of the sins of the land of Israel in a day
    3. Apparently, there is a connection between the removal of the sin of the land by the inscription engraved on the Stone in a day and the taking away of Joshua's sins symbolized by the removal of his filthy clothes
    4. The seven eyes on the stone are explained in the next vision, the vision of the Lampstand with Seven Lamps [Zec 4:10]

The Horses Report to the Angel of Yahweh

The horses were sent by Yahweh to go throughout the earth [Zec 1:10] and having gone throughout the earth, reported to the Angel of Yahweh that the earth was at peace [Zec 1]

And they reported to the Angel of Yahweh, who was standing among the myrtle trees, “We have gone throughout the earth and found the whole world at rest and in peace.”

It is very significant that the horses which were sent out by Yahweh reported to the Angel of Yahweh. This means either that

  1. The Angel of Yahweh to whom they were reporting was the Yahweh who sent them to go throughout the earth or that
  2. There is such a relationship between Yahweh and the the Angel of Yahweh so that though Yahweh God Most High sent the horses to investigate the state of things in the earth, they could report to the Angel of Yahweh and it wouldn't be wrong.

The Angel of Yahweh Prays to God For Jerusalem

Then the Angel of Yahweh said, “Yahweh of Hosts, how long will you withhold mercy from Jerusalem and from the towns of Judah, which you have been angry with these seventy years?” 13So Yahweh spoke kind and comforting words to the angel who talked with me. [Zec 1:12-13]

The Angel of Yahweh is not pleased to hear that the earth is at peace. From his words following after this,

  1. Since his interest is in Jerusalem's restoration, it means that the restoration of Jerusalem means trouble for the world.

Considering the kinsman redeemer laws, and the mind your business principle of scriptures, that the Angel of Yahweh prays for Jerusalem as he does means that he is over Israel.

  • It means that he is the Angel God appointed over Israel.
  • It means that he is the Captain of the Hosts of Yahweh [cf., Dan 12:1]

If he wasn't, then on what basis is he being so personally concerned about Jerusalem and God's people so much so that God answers him kindly?

God answering the Angel with kind and comforting words show that this Angel indeed has the right to be concerned for Jersualem and God's people.

Is The AoY the Same as the Guiding Angel, "...who spoke to me"?

Zec 1:12-13 seems to suggest that they are the same.

Yahweh Speaks Kindly and Comfortingly To the Angel

[Zec 1:13]

God's kind and comforting words to the Angel means that

  1. God is sympathetic towards this Angel
  2. The fulfilment of the request of the Angel of Yahweh is personal.
  3. The Angel of Yahweh has the right to be personally involved with Jersualem and God's people.
